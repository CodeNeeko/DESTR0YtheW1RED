ARJUN’S MELTDOWN IN THE KURUKSHETRA
Arjun is everyone of us who’s been paralyzed by a moral crises, torn between duty and conscience. Existential Despair

ENTERS KRISHNA
The soul never dies. The bodies might perish but their souls are immortal. [Perspective]
Cosmic Truth : Life and death are a illusion in the grand scheme.

ARJUN IS STILL STUCK BECAUSE HE’S STILL GOT HIS DUTY
Krishna reminds him that as a member of the warrior class Kshatriya, it is his dharma to fight.
Not out of personal desire but because it’s simply what he was born to do.

Everyday we face this dilemma, questioning our roles, purpose, duty but we’ve got job in this world and whether we hate it or love it, ignoring isn’t an option we’ve got to fulfil it.
Should you follow your dream or fulfil responsibilities ?
Krishna says that fulfil your role even if it sucks.
Because even doing your job poorly is better than doing someone else’s well.

DUTY ISN'T ABOUT PERSONAL GAIN, ITS ABOUT COSMIN ORDER.

[Desire] Hooking onto the material world.
Desire keeps us locked in the cycle of dharma. Fame, money, sex are all just fuel to this fire of desire.
And the result ? We’re trapped in the world of illusion, left endlessly chasing shadows, repeating the same mistakes over and over-“bondage of action”
We cant escape it if our every move is driven by wanting something in return.

KRISHNA OFFERS A WAY OUT
Act without attachment. Your obligation is to the action and never to its fruits.
So what you are suppose to do, without caring about the outcome.

[Devotion] Krishna talks about devotion that expects nothing in return. True Bhakti is selfless.

Arjun in his moral paralysis is now being told that the ultimate act of devotion isn’t kneeling in front of god hoping for blessings. Its living your life with such devotion to duty and righteousness that you cease to exist in the equation.
Vanish into the very act of service.

KRISHNA REVEALS HIMSELF
Not as the human figure, but as a full blown avatar into an infinite form, encompassing countless arms, mouth filling the entire universe.
Arjun could see every god and creature within him and it’s not pretty. Its terrifying.
This is Krishna revealing his role not only as the creator but also the destroyer of worlds.

In this moment of pure terror, Arjun grasps the Krishna’s message. Krishna is the cosmic force.
Time, death, creation, all of it.
And as much as Arjun wanted to avoid the battle, he realizes the choice was never his. The battle is already decided.
Krishna tells him, that these men are already dead, destroyed by the divine will.

Arjun like every other human is just an instrument of the cosmos.
His role isn’t to question the outcome; its to do his duty.
Rise up and fight, Krishna commands.
The fate of the world does not hinge upon your hesitation, it was written long before you were born.

SO WHAT DOES ARJUN DO ?
HE picks up his bow. But this time, he is no longer weighed down by doubt or fear. He knows now that he is just a cog in the grand cosmic machine, all he can do is play his part.

Krishna took apart the idea that your action alone shape the world around you.
You act, work but the grand design ? That’s out of your hands.

Krishna breaks down the essence of what drivees human behavior. He introduces the concept of three gunas :
Sattva (goodness)
Rajas (passion)
Tamas (ignorance)

These qualities influence our thoughts, actions and desires. Every human is governed by the combination of these force, rise above them.
You can’t be reactive, you need to recognize which guna is influencing you at any given moment of time.
Only can you start to control your actions and detach from the chaos of the material world.
This is the challenge that keeps you trapped in the cycles of desire, anger and ignorance.

ARJUN GETS READY TO FIGHT. KRISHNA GOES DEEPER INTO THE MECHANICS OF SELF MASTERY.
The goal of human existence is to rise above the illusions of the material world. To see the divine truth lurking underneath the regular life.
The three gunas are always there, but self mastery comes when you can recognize their influence and detach from it.

Arjun’s battle is just a metaphor for the battles you face every single day.

[Selfless Action] Krishna tells Arjun that the key to inner peace to breaking free from the endless cycle of desire and disappointment is to act without expecting anything in return.
Act for the sake of the action itself.

Live every second of your life in conscience awareness of something greater than your self, which is an antidote to ego driver culture.

Realize that you are part of a much bigger story.
