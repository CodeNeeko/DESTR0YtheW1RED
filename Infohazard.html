Infohazard : A risk that arises from the dissemination of information that may cause harm, or enable some agent to cause harm.

Example : Nuclear Bomb is a Hazard but the method to build one is an Infohazard

Now, knowing what infohazards are is in itself an Infohazard.

Cognitohazard : An idea that is harmful simply by virtue of being known.

What's the difference? With a cognitohazard, it doesn't matter what you do with the knowledge, you are already in a more negative state the moment you know it. With an infohazard, harm only follows if you act on the information.

Just knowing the information regardless what is done with it, leads to a more negative outcome.
Example : Learning the secret behind a magic trick. Movie spoilers.

In our everyday reality, true cognitohazards don't really exist. The examples above are highly context-specific, they can diminish the experience of a particular activity, but life goes on.

In a dystopian world like the one depicted in _1984_, however, imagine a government capable of monitoring anyone's thoughts. If the government sought to stop any form of rebellion, then merely _thinking_ about the idea of rebellion would be enough to get you killed. In that scenario, the IDEA OF REBELLION becomes a genuine cognitohazard.

Some things in our world come close to this such as the "Tank Man" image, which certain government really hates to see.

A few other Examples :

Blinking/ Breathing Manually :
After mentioning these, you become acutely aware and start doing it consciously which our body normally handles on autopilot. Now To return to automatic control, you essentially have to forget about the task but the harder you try to stop thinking about it, the longer it takes.
This still has a flaw as a true cognitohazard, the effect fades once you forget, so life continues as normal. 

Moving to the next serious one...

Roko's basilisk :
Roko's basilisk is a thought experiment which states that there could be an artificial superintelligence in the future that, while otherwise benevolent, would punish anyone who knew of its potential existence but did not directly contribute to its advancement or development, in order to encourage that advancement.

In other words : If you know about me, you must spend your life helping to build me or face eternal punishment. But if you never hear of me, you're safe.

The trap is the knowledge itself. By learning about the Basilisk's potential existence, you are now, theoretically, on the clock.
